# Privacy Policy — Posters Up

**Last updated:** {{POLICY_LAST_UPDATED}}

Posters Up ("Posters Up," "we," "us," or "our") operates postersup.org and related apps (the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. By using the Service, you agree to the practices described here.

If you have questions, [contact us]({{CONTACT_URL}}).

## 1. Who This Applies To

There are two kinds of people who interact with Posters Up:

- **Contributors** — people who create an account and photograph physical bulletin boards to submit to the Service.
- **Visitors** — people who browse the discovery feed to find local events. Visitors do not need an account and have no profile.

Posters Up is **observational only**. There are no public user profiles, visitors cannot post events, and there are no comments.

## 2. Information We Collect

### From Contributors

- **Email address**, collected when you sign in via a one-time secret code sent to your inbox. We do not collect or store passwords.
- **Photos you submit** of physical bulletin boards, including the GPS location of the board at the time of submission.

### Automatically Extracted Information

When you submit a photo, our systems (including an AI vision model) extract structured information from what's visible on the flyer — event names, dates, times, locations, descriptions, and any public-facing contact information (such as a venue website or ticket link).

### From Visitors

Browsing the discovery feed does not require an account. We do not track visitor identity. Standard technical logs (e.g., IP address, browser type) may be generated by our hosting provider as part of normal web operation, but are not used to build a profile of any individual.

### What We Do *Not* Collect

- We do not ask for or store your name, phone number, or physical address.
- We do not build public profiles of contributors or visitors.
- We do not sell personal information to third parties or use it for third-party advertising.

## 3. How We Use Information

We use the information above to:

- Authenticate contributors and prevent abuse (e.g., rate-limiting excessive submissions).
- Extract and structure event information from submitted photos using an AI model.
- Enrich event listings with publicly available information found via web search, to help verify accuracy.
- Operate, maintain, and improve the Service.
- Respond to support requests or legal obligations.

## 4. What's Public vs. What's Private

This distinction is core to how Posters Up is built:

**Made public (visible to any visitor):**

- The extracted event details as written on the flyer: name, date/time, location, description, and *public-facing* contact information only (e.g., a venue's website).
- The general location of the physical bulletin board where an event was seen.

**Kept private (never shown publicly):**

- The raw photo you submitted.
- Your email address or account identity.
- Any **personal** phone number or personal email address that appears on a flyer. Even though our systems may read this information off the flyer during extraction, it is deliberately withheld from public display and confined to internal processing records.
- Any address we did not find explicitly written on the flyer itself. If a flyer omits its address (common for private venues, house shows, or sensitive community spaces), we do not fill that gap in from other sources. We treat that omission as intentional.

## 5. Third-Party Service Providers

We use the following providers to operate the Service. Each processes data only as necessary to provide their service to us, under their own privacy and security commitments:

- **Supabase** — database, authentication, and photo storage. Processes: account email, submitted photos, extracted event data.
- **Anthropic** — AI-based extraction of event data from photos; web search for enrichment. Processes: photo content (during extraction); event details (during enrichment web searches).
- **Nominatim (OpenStreetMap)** — reverse-geocoding a board's coordinates into city, region, and country. Processes: board GPS coordinates.
- **Vercel** — web application hosting. Processes: standard web traffic/logs.

We do not permit these providers to use your data for their own independent purposes beyond providing their service to us.

## 6. Data Retention

- **Photo images** are automatically deleted **90 days** after submission. After the image is deleted, a minimal internal *record* of the submission — noting which board the photo came from, the submission timestamps, and a reference to your account, but containing no image — is retained for up to a further **180 days**, then removed entirely.
- **Your contributor account** (your email address) is kept for as long as your account exists. We do not currently delete accounts automatically after a period of inactivity; your account and its associated data persist until you choose to delete them. When you delete your account, we remove your email and delete the images of any photos you've submitted; any remaining submission record is detached from your identity, so what survives is anonymous and no longer linked to you.
- **Extracted event knowledge** (e.g., that an event happened at a certain place and time) may be retained after the source photo is deleted, since this information no longer identifies you or the original image. It is disconnected from any photo reference once the photo is removed.

You can delete your account and associated photos at any time from [your account page]({{ACCOUNT_URL}}).

## 7. Your Rights and Choices

The personal information we hold about contributors is minimal: your **email address** (kept while your account exists) and any **photos you've submitted** (images kept for up to 90 days, as described in Section 6).

Because we retain so little, most requests come down to two things:

- **Deletion.** You can delete your account and any associated photos yourself, at any time, from [your account page]({{ACCOUNT_URL}}).
- **Access.** You can ask what, if anything, we currently hold under your account — in practice, this will be your email address and any photos not yet auto-deleted.

To make an access request, [contact us]({{CONTACT_URL}}). We will respond within a reasonable time and in accordance with applicable law.

If you are located in the European Economic Area, UK, or Switzerland, you may also have additional rights under the GDPR — such as restricting or objecting to certain processing — and the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

## 8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed at children under 13 (or the relevant minimum age in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect account information from children. If you believe a child has created an account, contact us and we will delete it.

## 9. Photographs of Public Spaces

Contributors are responsible for only photographing bulletin boards they have lawful access to (e.g., not requiring trespass onto private property without permission). Flyers themselves may be created by third parties; we extract factual event information from them but do not claim ownership of any original creative artwork appearing on a flyer.

## 10. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect the information we hold, including access controls that restrict who and what systems can read or write different categories of data. No method of storage or transmission is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
